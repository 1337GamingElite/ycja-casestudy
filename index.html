The SITUATION - #3

When Mark got his driver's license, he could not wait to take his friends out for a drive. On a Saturday evening, Mark and a group of his friends piled into a car and headed out from Edmonton toward Sherwood Park. When the vehicle pulled up to a stoplight, a sports car filled with another group of teens pulled up alongside. As both drivers waited for the light to turn green, the sports car accelerated quickly. Not to be outdone, Mark stepped on the gas and, with squealing tires, his car followed in hot pursuit. The two cars sped down the road, reaching speeds well above the speed limit. Swerving to avoid a collision, Mark's car hit the curb, flew across a lawn, and smashed into the front of a house.


Luckily, Mark and his friends recieved only minor injuries; however, the front end of the car he was driving was destroyed and the house suffered several thousands of dollars in damage.

The FACTS

Key details of the crime:

  • Perpetrator: Mark (recently acquired a drivers license)
  • Crime: Speeding | Dangerous Driving | Property Damage
  • Victim: Owner of damaged property | Mark and his group of friends in the vehicle

During a Saturday night, an attempted street race happened between Mark and an unknown group of teenagers who had left the scene. This resulted in Mark crashing his vehicle. This crash caused minor injuries among Mark and his accomplices, but also damaged the front of a resident's house. The resulting damage was estimated to cost thousands of dollars.

One key detail is that Mark has recently got his driver's license. With this information, we can safely assume that this was Mark's first time offense and that he is young enough to be charged under the Youth Criminal Justice Act (16 years or older in order to be eligible to recieve a Class 5 Driver's License).
